Symptom: 
OSPF neighbors in Area 3 (R3, R9, R10) were stuck in '2-WAY/DROTHER' state.

Analysis:
In a Broadcast network, routers only reach 'FULL' state with the DR and BDR. 
Because the 'Wait' timer was set to 80s (due to doubled timers) and the 
OSPF election is non-preemptive, no router was claiming the DR role. R3 remained in 'WAITING' while claiming the DR title 
while neighbors didn't see it as a leader.

Resolution:
1. Confirmed that the Hello & Dead intervals and MTU attributes matched b/n all routers in the area.
2. Performed a 'clear ip ospf process' on all Area 3 routers.
3. Cycled the interface on R3 and waited for the 80s 'Wait' timer to expire 
   before bringing up R9/R10, ending up with R10 & R9 as DR & BDR, respectively, accordingly with their router IDs.
